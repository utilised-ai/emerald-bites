The Brazen Head

Ireland's Oldest Pub - Established 1198

4.5/5 (843 reviews)

Step through the doors of The Brazen Head and you're walking into over 800 years of Irish history. Established in 1198, this is Ireland's oldest pub, a place where time seems to stand still while the world outside continues to change.

Located in the heart of Dublin, The Brazen Head offers not just authentic Irish cuisine but a genuine slice of Irish culture, with traditional music sessions, storytelling evenings, and a warm, convivial atmosphere that has welcomed locals and visitors alike for centuries.

Our Review

The Brazen Head isn't just a restaurant or a pub—it's a living museum, a cultural institution, and a culinary landmark all rolled into one. While many historic establishments rest on their laurels, trading solely on their heritage, The Brazen Head continues to deliver quality food and an authentic experience that keeps both locals and tourists coming back.

The pub's stone walls, wooden beams, and cozy nooks create an atmosphere that's both intimate and communal. On any given night, you might find yourself sharing a table with visitors from around the world, all drawn by the pub's legendary status and the promise of good food and better craic (Irish for fun and entertainment).

What truly sets The Brazen Head apart is how it balances its historic significance with a commitment to serving excellent traditional Irish cuisine. The kitchen takes pride in using locally sourced ingredients to create dishes that honor Ireland's culinary heritage while satisfying modern palates.

History & Cultural Significance

The Brazen Head's history is intertwined with that of Dublin itself. Originally established as a coaching inn in 1198, it has witnessed nearly a millennium of Irish history, from medieval times through the Viking and Norman invasions, the various rebellions against British rule, and the birth of the Irish Republic.

Over the centuries, the pub has welcomed many notable patrons, including revolutionary leaders like Robert Emmet and Wolfe Tone, and literary giants such as James Joyce, Jonathan Swift, and Brendan Behan. Joyce even mentioned The Brazen Head in his masterpiece "Ulysses."

Today, The Brazen Head continues its tradition as a gathering place for storytelling, music, and conviviality. The pub hosts regular traditional Irish music sessions that showcase the best of Ireland's musical heritage, as well as storytelling evenings where visitors can immerse themselves in Irish folklore and mythology.

Location & Directions

The Brazen Head is conveniently located in Dublin's historic center, just a short walk from many major attractions including Christ Church Cathedral, Guinness Storehouse, and Temple Bar.

Getting There

By Bus: Routes 25, 25a, 25b, 26, 66, 67 stop nearby at Merchant's Quay.

By Tram: The closest Luas stop is Four Courts on the Red Line, a 5-minute walk away.

By Foot: 10-minute walk from Temple Bar, 15-minute walk from Trinity College.
