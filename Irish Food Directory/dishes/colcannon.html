Colcannon

About This Dish

Colcannon is a beloved Irish comfort food that combines creamy mashed potatoes with kale or cabbage. This traditional dish is often served alongside ham or bacon, making it a perfect accompaniment to any Irish meal. The name comes from the Gaelic term "cál ceannann" meaning white-headed cabbage.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg potatoes, peeled and quartered
  • 1 head of cabbage or bunch of kale, finely chopped
  • 2 leeks, finely sliced
  • 250ml milk
  • 100g butter
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 4 spring onions, finely chopped
  • Optional: crispy bacon bits for garnish

Instructions

  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until tender (about 20 minutes).
  2. Meanwhile, cook the cabbage or kale in a separate pan until soft but still bright green.
  3. In another pan, sauté the leeks in a bit of butter until soft.
  4. Drain the potatoes and return to the pot. Add half the butter and warm milk.
  5. Mash until smooth and creamy.
  6. Fold in the cooked cabbage/kale and leeks.
  7. Season well with salt and pepper.
  8. Serve hot with a knob of butter melting in the center and sprinkled with spring onions.

Serving Suggestions

Traditionally served with Irish ham or bacon, colcannon can also be enjoyed as a side dish with any meat. For a modern twist, try it with grilled salmon or as a base for vegetarian dishes. Don't forget to make a well in the center for an extra knob of butter!
